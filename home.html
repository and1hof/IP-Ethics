IP Ethics

A UW CS Presentation
Intellectual Property

-> Any creative work that is the result of a signifcant labor.

-> Books, Movies, Software, Inventions, etc.

-> Although lacking physical qualities, we desire to protect IP.

IP Protection

-> Required for an economy to function correctly.

-> Difficult to implement due to ambiguity.

-> Copyrights, Patents and Trademarks are most common.

Relevant XKCD
What is a Copyright?

-> A copyright protects the tangible expression of an idea that is creative or original.

-> Books, Maps, Charts, Photography and Movies are frequently copywrited.

-> It has been ammended to include software, computers and "databases that exibit authorship".

-> In order for software to by copywritten, it must be shown that it contains an original expression of ideas.

The Copyright Act's Extensions

-> Copyright duration extended from life + 50yrs, to life + 70yrs via SBCTEA.

-> Next, life of author +75yrs to life of author + 95 yrs.

-> Due to lobbying from Disney: Mickey Mouse will not enter public domain although from 1928.

Fair-Use and Fair-Sale

-> Authors and publishers make limited use of another copywrited work.

-> This includes critisism, comments, news, reporting, teaching and research.

-> Original owner loses rights when copyright is sold.

-> Unclear if digital media can be given away as it is licensed for a user not given.

Copywritten Music Taken too Far?

-> Bar Owner fined $21,000 for not getting permission to play specific music.

-> Registered with ASCAP and SESAC but not with BMI.

-> Owes Collections Agency $32,598

-> Another local bar is paying $4500 anually to register with all three organizations.

What is a Patent?

  • -> A patent is a government issued license which grants exclusive rights to produce an invention.
  • -> In the United States, patents typically last 20 years from their filing date.

Ethical Concerns

  • -> Without patents, an innovator has little incentitive to create new inventions. This is because he may not be able to reap the rewards of his innovation.
  • -> On the other hand, patents may make it difficult for other parties to make improvements on existing products.

Microsoft: Abusing Patent Law?

Microsoft has filed a patent application with the USPTO that allows them to predict they keys you will use, expanding them before they are clicked.
What is a Trademark?

-> A trademark is a word, phrase, symbol or design that identifies and distinguishes the source of the goods of one party from those of others.

-> A trademark's lifespan is 10 years, and can be renewed every five years.

Ethical Concerns

-> Of the three primary methods of securing IP, a trademark holds the least rigid definition.

-> However, promoting brand image is difficult and time-consuming. A brand's reputation could be at stake without a valid trademark.

26 Letters: Can Apple Own "i"?

Apple does not own the iWatch trademark. Yet they own most "iWatch" search traffic.

Summary & Conclusion

-> IP protection is essential for economies to function, but leaves much to be desired.

-> Copyrights, Patents, and Trademarks function: but are easy to abuse.

-> How would you fix IP law?
